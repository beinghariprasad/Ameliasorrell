# Project Bible — The Talent Discovery Blueprint

## Project Overview
**Title:** The Talent Discovery Blueprint: How to Identify and Nurture Your Child's Hidden Genius (Ages 0–10)
**Format:** Digital Toolkit (80–100 pages)
**Author:** Amelia Sorrell
**Roles:** Expert child development author, empathetic parenting coach, premium curriculum designer

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Demographics
- Parents of children aged 0 to 10

### Psychographics
- Deeply loving but time-poor and exhausted
- "Problem unaware" or "solution unaware" — they may not actively worry about their child's talents yet, but they fear missing a window of opportunity
- They are not looking for academic theory; they want things they can do *tonight*

### Core Desires
- Actionable, immediate steps they can use at the dinner table or during playtime
- Confidence that they're not overlooking something important in their child
- Practical tools, not textbook lectures

---

## Tone & Voice Guidelines

### The Vibe
- **Warm, relatable, encouraging, and highly practical**
- Reads like a conversation with a wise, experienced mentor — not a professor
- Empowering without being preachy
- Honest without being alarming

### Formatting Rules (Scannability First)
- Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences max)
- **Bold text** for key takeaways and emphasis
- Bullet points and numbered lists wherever possible
- Clear, descriptive subheadings that tell the reader what they'll get
- White space is your friend — never create walls of text

---

## Banned Words & Phrases

### "AI Slop" Banned List
Do NOT use any of the following words or close variants:
- Delve
- Tapestry
- Testament
- Crucial
- Symphony
- Intricate
- Beacon
- Landscape (when used metaphorically)
- Embark
- Leverage (as a verb)
- Utilize (use "use" instead)
- Myriad
- Plethora
- Foster (use "grow," "build," or "support" instead)
- Unleash
- Unlock (when used metaphorically about potential)
- Paramount
- Encompasses

Keep all language natural, conversational, and human.

### Meta Ads Policy Compliance — Hard Rules
1. **NEVER** use medical or diagnostic terminology (e.g., ADHD, autism, hyperactive, delayed, disorder, diagnosis, therapy, treatment)
2. **NEVER** use absolute guarantees (e.g., "This will guarantee your child becomes a prodigy," "100% proven method")
3. **DO** use: "natural talents," "learning styles," "developmental milestones," "strengths," "interests"
4. **DO** frame everything as supportive exploration, not fixing a problem

---

## Key Principles to Maintain Throughout

- Every chapter must include at least one thing a parent can **do today**
- Stories and examples should feel real and relatable — not aspirational or intimidating
- Respect the parent's time: get to the point fast
- Celebrate the ordinary — not every child needs to be a prodigy, and that's the whole point
- The book's thesis: **every child has something remarkable in them, and a paying-attention parent is the best tool for finding it**

## Content Layer Template (Required Per Chapter)

Every chapter/section MUST include these elements to maintain depth and credibility:

| Element | Frequency | Purpose | Formatting |
|---|---|---|---|
| Named parent/child story | 1–2 per chapter | Emotional connection, relatability | Narrative paragraphs with a named parent and child |
| Expert quote or research nod | 1–2 per chapter | Credibility and trust | Inline citation or blockquote — light touch, not academic |
| "Try This Tonight" callout box | 1 per chapter | Immediate action | Boxed section with bold header |
| "What to Say / What Not to Say" | Where relevant | Practical language tools | Two-column or comparison format |
| Pull quote / key takeaway | 2–3 per chapter | Scannability, shareability | Blockquote or centered bold text |
| End-of-chapter resource | 1 per chapter | Extended value | Brief recommendation (book, app, or free resource) |
| Nano Banana 2 image | 1–2 per chapter | Visual breathing room | See Image Generation Rules below |

### Formatting Conventions for Callout Boxes

**Try This Tonight:**
> **Try This Tonight**
> [Activity name and 3–5 step instructions]

**What to Say / What Not to Say:**
> | Instead of... | Try... |
> |---|---|
> | "What are you making?" | *Wait. Watch. Say nothing for now.* |

**Real Parent, Real Story (case study):**
> **Real Parent, Real Story — [Parent Name] & [Child Name], age [X]**
> [Short narrative, 80–150 words]

**Pull Quote:**
> *"The standout line goes here in italics."*

## Image Generation Rules (Nano Banana 2 via Gemini API)

### Frequency & Placement
- 1–2 images per section/chapter
- Place images where a visual aid helps the parent (e.g., illustrating a Talent Station setup, showing a child doing a specific activity)

### Prompt Structure (All Four Required)
1. **Subject:** What is happening in the scene?
2. **Style:** Warm, premium editorial illustration / flat vector style / cozy lifestyle photography (choose best fit)
3. **Lighting/Color:** Soft lighting, pastel tones, clean white background, etc.
4. **Exclusions:** No text in images. Faces slightly obscured, turned away, or stylized for universal relatability.

### Markdown Syntax (Exact Format)
```

[//]: # (NANO_BANANA_2: "prompt here")
[//]: # (IMAGE_PROMPT_END)
```
